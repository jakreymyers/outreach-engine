# Single Conversation Synthesis Prompt

**Purpose:** Extract key insights, quotes, and signals from a single customer conversation while everything is fresh.

---

## Context to Provide

Before using this prompt, prepare:

1. **Conversation notes** - Raw notes, transcript, or memory dump
2. **Conversation type** - Validation interview, sales discovery, casual chat, etc.
3. **What you were trying to learn** - Your main questions going in

---

## The Prompt

Copy everything below the line and paste into your AI tool:

---

I just finished a customer conversation and need to synthesize the key insights while everything is fresh.

**Conversation with:**
[Name, role, company/project]

**Conversation type:**
[Validation interview / Sales discovery / Casual chat / Other]

**Duration:**
[How long was the conversation?]

**What I was trying to learn:**
1. [Main question 1]
2. [Main question 2]
3. [Main question 3]

**My notes/transcript:**
```
[PASTE YOUR NOTES OR TRANSCRIPT HERE - can be messy, that's fine]
```

**Analyze this conversation and provide:**

### 1. Executive Summary
2-3 sentences capturing the most important takeaway from this conversation.

### 2. Key Quotes
Extract 5-8 direct quotes (or close paraphrases) that are:
- Evidence of the problem existing
- Surprising or unexpected
- Emotionally charged
- Useful for messaging/copy
- Indicative of willingness to pay

Format: "Quote" - Context for why it matters

### 3. Problem Intensity Assessment
On a scale of 1-5, how intensely does this person experience the problem?
- What evidence supports this rating?
- What would change the rating?

### 4. Current State Analysis
- How do they currently solve/cope with this problem?
- What's working about their current approach?
- What's not working?
- How much time/money does this cost them?

### 5. Signal Assessment
Rate each signal category (0-3):

| Signal | Score | Evidence |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Problem is real | | |
| Problem is painful | | |
| They're actively seeking solutions | | |
| They would pay to solve this | | |
| They have authority/budget | | |
| Timing is right | | |

### 6. Surprises & New Insights
What did I learn that I didn't expect? What challenges or changes my assumptions?

### 7. Gaps & Follow-Up Questions
What didn't I learn that I should have? What questions should I ask next time or follow up on?

### 8. Customer Profile
- Is this person my ideal customer? Why or why not?
- If they're a great fit, what makes them one?
- If they're not, what's missing?

### 9. Action Items
What should I do as a result of this conversation?
- Immediate (within 24 hours)
- Short-term (this week)
- Future (follow-up, nurture)

### 10. Usable Assets
- Quotes for marketing/sales copy
- Objections to prepare for
- Feature requests or ideas mentioned
- Referral potential (did they mention anyone else?)

## Example Output

**Conversation with:** Sarah Chen, founder of TaskFlow (project management tool)
**Type:** Validation interview
**Duration:** 35 minutes
**Trying to learn:** Is validation painful? How does she do it? Would she pay?

**Notes:**
```
Started building TaskFlow 6 months ago. Was eng at Stripe before.
Built it because "I had the problem myself" - managing personal projects.
Launched on PH, got 3 signups. "Devastating." Spent whole weekend refreshing the page.
Never talked to users before building. "I figured if I have this problem, others must too."
When asked how she validated: "I didn't really... I just built it."
Now trying to figure out what went wrong. Reading a lot of content about validation.
Currently doing "talking to people in Reddit threads" but feels "random and scattered."
Wish she had "a system" - said it multiple times.
Mentioned she's spent maybe $2k on ads trying to get users. Nothing worked.
When asked about willingness to pay: "I'd pay $50/month for something that would have saved me 6 months."
Has another idea but "terrified to start building" because of TaskFlow experience.
Wants to do it right this time but doesn't know what "right" looks like.
```

---

### 1. Executive Summary

Sarah is a high-intent validation prospect. She experienced the problem firsthand (6 months building, 3 users), now has awareness that validation matters, but lacks a systematic process. She explicitly stated willingness to pay $50/month and is motivated by fear of repeating the same mistake.

---

### 2. Key Quotes

1. **"I figured if I have this problem, others must too."**
   - Classic founder assumption trap. Useful for messaging - shows the mindset we're addressing.

2. **"Devastating."** (describing launch day)
   - Emotional intensity. Shows the real cost of skipping validation.

3. **"I spent the whole weekend refreshing the Product Hunt page."**
   - Vivid detail. Great for empathy-building content.

4. **"I didn't really... I just built it."** (on validation)
   - Moment of self-awareness. Shows recognition of the gap.

5. **"I'd pay $50/month for something that would have saved me 6 months."**
   - Direct willingness to pay. Quantified value.

6. **"Terrified to start building"** (new idea)
   - Emotional state. Fear is a motivator.

7. **"Random and scattered"** (on current approach)
   - Pain with current process. Opportunity for "system" positioning.

8. **"I wish I had a system."** (said multiple times)
   - Feature/positioning insight. "System" language resonates.

---

### 3. Problem Intensity Assessment

**Rating: 5/5 (Acute)**

**Evidence:**
- Spent 6 months building something that failed
- Emotionally affected ("devastating")
- Actively trying to learn (reading content)
- Paralyzed on next project (won't start building)
- Explicit financial loss ($2K on ads)
- Clear before/after moment ("I wish I had")

**What would lower this rating:**
- If this was theoretical or hadn't happened yet
- If she wasn't planning to start another project
- If she'd already solved it with another tool

---

### 4. Current State Analysis

**How she currently copes:**
- Reading validation content online
- "Talking to people in Reddit threads"
- No formal process or tools

**What's working:**
- She's trying (awareness is there)
- Has access to communities where users are

**What's not working:**
- "Random and scattered" - no system
- No way to track or organize learnings
- No framework for knowing when she's "done"

**Cost:**
- Time: 6 months on failed project
- Money: $2K+ on ads that didn't work
- Emotional: "Terrified" to start next thing
- Opportunity: Not building what could work

---

### 5. Signal Assessment

| Signal | Score | Evidence |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Problem is real | 3/3 | Lived it. 6 months lost. |
| Problem is painful | 3/3 | "Devastating." Terrified to repeat. |
| Actively seeking solutions | 3/3 | Reading content, talking to people, took this call |
| Would pay to solve | 3/3 | Explicitly said $50/month |
| Has authority/budget | 3/3 | Sole founder, already spent $2K |
| Timing is right | 3/3 | Between projects, actively deciding |

**Total: 18/18** - This is as strong a signal as you'll find.

---

### 6. Surprises & New Insights

1. **The fear of starting again is real** - She has a new idea but won't start building. This is a specific emotional state worth targeting.

2. **"System" language** - She used this word multiple times unprompted. Strong messaging hook.

3. **$50/month framing** - She framed willingness to pay in terms of what she'd have paid to prevent the problem, not what she'd pay for a tool. Interesting positioning angle.

4. **Reddit engagement isn't working** - She's trying validation but it feels ineffective. Our tool needs to feel different from "just talking to people."

---

### 7. Gaps & Follow-Up Questions

**Didn't learn:**
- What's the new idea? (Could help personalize follow-up)
- Has she tried any validation tools? (Competitive intel)
- What would "done with validation" look like for her? (Success criteria)
- Would she want to use this with a co-founder or alone? (Use case)

**Follow-up questions for next conversation:**
- "What would need to be true for you to feel confident starting to build?"
- "If you had evidence that your idea was worth building, what would that evidence look like?"

---

### 8. Customer Profile

**Is she ideal? YES**

**What makes her ideal:**
- First-time founder (core target)
- Experienced the exact problem (high awareness)
- Currently between projects (right timing)
- Technical (can build, validation is the gap)
- Self-funded but willing to invest
- Clear before/after moment

**What could be better:**
- She's solo - team founders might have higher budgets
- She hasn't tried other tools - harder to differentiate
- Side project energy - might deprioritize if life gets busy

---

### 9. Action Items

**Immediate (within 24 hours):**
- Send thank you email referencing "system" language
- Include link to trial signup
- Offer to walk through first validation together

**Short-term (this week):**
- Follow up if she hasn't started trial in 3 days
- Check if she has questions

**Future:**
- Great case study candidate if she succeeds
- Ask for referral to other founders who've had similar experiences
- Feature testimonial opportunity if it works well

---

### 10. Usable Assets

**Marketing quotes:**
- "I figured if I have this problem, others must too." (before state)
- "I'd pay $50/month for something that would have saved me 6 months." (value proof)
- "Devastating." (emotional hook)

**Objections to prepare for:**
- "I can just talk to people on Reddit" - Need to show why that's "random and scattered"
- Price objection - Frame against cost of another failed project

**Feature ideas:**
- "System" positioning - Structure and process, not just tools
- Clear "done with validation" signal
- Progress tracking (she felt "scattered")

**Referral potential:**
- Likely knows other ex-Stripe founders building things
- Ask: "Do you know anyone else who's been through a similar experience?"

---

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Synthesize immediately** - Do this within an hour of the conversation while details are fresh.

2. **Include the messy notes** - The AI can find patterns in scattered thoughts. Don't clean up too much first.

3. **Be honest about weak signals** - It's better to know someone isn't a fit than to over-interpret weak interest.

4. **Save the output** - These syntheses become input for pattern extraction across multiple conversations.

5. **Note your emotional read** - The transcript doesn't capture tone. Add observations about how engaged/excited they seemed.

## Follow-Up Prompts

**"I have a transcript instead of notes. Extract the same insights."**

**"This conversation was negative - they're not a fit. What can I still learn?"**

**"I think I led the witness during this conversation. Help me identify where and discount those signals."**

**"Compare this synthesis to my last 3. What patterns are emerging?"**
